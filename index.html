ABOUT ME

My name is Tad and two of my favorite things are music and technology. They have both been an incredibly important part of my life ever since I was young. I grew up mainly on Christian music until I was around 6 which is when I started to discover my own taste in music; at the time this mainly consisted of minecraft parodies since I was like 6 years old. The first band I really got into was The Beatles which I was introduced to by my Papa, I was around 8 or 9 when I first started listening to them and they left a lasting impression on my taste in music although I don't listen to them all that much anymore. For several years I considered the beatles to be my favorite band until about 8th grade when I discovered Death Grips. I was recommended Death Grips by a friend who I don't talk to anymore. The first thing I thought when I heard my first Death Grips song was "wow this sucks," after about one week of listening to Death Grips off and on I realized that I loved it. The style of Death Grips was something I had never previously heard of before, Experimental Hip Hop wasn't even a word in my vocabulary. Death Grips helped me get through a really rough time in 2019 which is another reason I love them so much. Later on in life I got heavily into Modest Mouse and Weezer which yet again helped shaped my taste in music. Also currently I play the Clarinet in high school marching band.



Technology has shaped my world ever since I was little. I grew up on the Wii and Ds Lite, the Wii is still one of my favorite consoles of all time; I have so many fond memories of playing games on the Wii with family and friends. I had an Xbox 360 at one point which I stupidly traded the Wii for, I mainly just used it to play Minecraft and Call Of Duty. Currently I own a Nintendo Switch and a Retron 2 which is a system that has two slots for Snes and Nes games. My favorite game on the Snes is A Link To The Past although I don't own it physically. Overall I'm a pretty big nerd when it comes to technology, I hope to go to college one day for either Computer Science or Music.
